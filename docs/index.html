Review the editorial board of an international journal

Find a journal in your discipline that describes itself as international. Review how globally representative its editorial board is.

This prompt helps you to identify gaps in representation that can often go unnoticed.

Review who’s on the editorial boards

Of the last three journals you published in. How representative are each? Racially? Geographically? By gender? Bonus points for editorial boards you participate on.

This prompt helps you to identify gaps in representation that can often go unnoticed.

Review who you’re citing

For the last article you published, review the authors you cited. How many are women? People of color?

This prompt helps you to identify gaps in representation that can often go unnoticed.

Remove paywalled citations

For your last article, check to see which citations you referenced are not freely accessible without a subscription. Unpaywall is a tool for this. Consider the impact on your work if you couldn’t have used these paywalled sources.

This prompt helps you to reflect on the limitations placed on scholars without access to journal subscriptions.

Run your last abstract through a translator

Translate the text into another language, then have the tool convert that translation back to the original language. Google offers a tool for doing this. The lack of accuracy is the point!

This prompt helps you understand some of the challenges of reading and publishing in a non-native language.

Then give it away.

This prompt helps you understand what it’s like to buy a paper only to discover the contents aren’t helpful.
